Solution: So You Think You Can Spell
Answer: BUMBLEBEE

Written by Lennart Jansson, Rahul Sridhar, Ben Yang

This puzzle is an homage to the Spelling Bee game published by the New York Times.

The rules for the standard Spelling Bee game are explained on the NYT site and are as follows:

  • Words must contain at least 4 letters.
  • Words must include the center letter.
  • Obscure words, proper nouns and hyphenated words are not accepted.
  • Letters can be used more than once.

Each word is worth some number of points:

  • 4-letter words are worth 1 point each.
  • Longer words earn 1 point per letter.
  • Each puzzle includes at least one ‚Äúpangram‚Äù which uses every letter. These are worth 7 extra points!

Each level has nine ranks based strictly on a percentage of possible total points attainable. Genius is generally set at 70%.

This puzzle contains seven Spelling Bee levels each of which (besides the first) contains some kind of twist. You can move on to the next level once you reach Genius in the current level.

Once you reach Genius in the final level, you're given the answer to the puzzle: BUMBLEBEE.

Level 1: Standard Spelling Bee

This level uses normal Spelling Bee rules to allow a chance for puzzlers to familiarize themselves with the rules and scoring. It does however have an unusually large set of valid words.

Left pangram: WEIRDLY

Right pangram: LOWBALLED

Level 3: Free Vowels

Words in this level are allowed to use any of the 7 consonants present as well as any vowels. Words must still use at least 4 (not necessarily unique) included consonants and must still contain the center letter. There are no pangrams in this level.

Level 4: Doubled Center

The required center letter is secretly doubled when the word is entered. The resulting word must be a valid English word. For example, you may enter BOTLED in the right version since that will be transformed into BOTTLED.

Left pangram: FLATFOTED

Right pangram: LIBRETO

Level 5: Center Swap

Every time you enter a word, that word's last letter swaps places to become the new center letter. This allows access to a greater number of valid words.

Left pangram: KVETCHED

Right pangram: WIZARDRY

Level 6: Pok√©mon

While regular words are accepted, the rankings thresholds are so high that they're mostly useless. As clued by the flavortext in the rankings dialog, each side has six hidden Pok√©mon that can be made from the given letters. Each is worth 400 points and finding all six is necessary to reach Genius.

Left Pok√©mon: AGGRON, ARON, GENGAR, GOGOAT, RATTATA, TORTERRA

Level 7: Letter Increment

Every time you enter a word, any outer letters that it uses are shifted forward by one (A becomes B, B becomes C, and so on). If there are multiple identical outer letters, only one is shifted forward. The standard pangram bonus is awarded if the word uses all unique visible letters.

There are multiple possible solution paths to hit Genius, but the key is to carefully manage the set of vowels you have available at any given point. For example, while it may be tempting to start off the left side with the pangram CHALICED (or even better, CICHLIDAE), this increments too many vowels at once, leaving you with fewer to use for future words.

Left/Right Answers

The left and right answers needed for this round's meta can be found by extracting the initial center letter of each level. Doing so gives us REMOVAL and DAYTRIP for the left and right answers, respectively.

Author’s Notes

A few of us regularly do the NYT Spelling Bee together. When we get stuck on finding more actual words (or at least what the New York Times considers actual words), we often point out interesting non-words that could be made, including Pok√©mon, proper nouns, and phrases. Parts of this puzzle offer a glimpse into what that's like. In fact, level 2 was written by looking at the letter sets, pretending we were stuck, and throwing the usual silly suggestions out there.
