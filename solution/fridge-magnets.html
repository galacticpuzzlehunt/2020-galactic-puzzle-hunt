Solution: Fridge Magnets
Answer: DOMAIN

Written by Chris Jones

The title and design of the puzzle are meant to evoke magnetic word tiles that can be arranged to make funny phrases.

Which phrases should we make? The white word tiles look like they come from well-known clichés/idioms. For example we can make "pride cometh before the fall" using the tiles in the left puzzle.

However, these clichés don't quite fit in the blanks below! We need to add the green words in the indicated spaces, thereby modifying the clichés into silly joke versions. For example, "history teachers repeat themselves" is a pun on "history repeats itself".

Left side Right side
don't sweat the small stuff; bigger droplets are more effective when one door closes, knock
pride month cometh before the fall newfoundland is an island
a bird in the hand is very angry take french fries with a grain of salt
life isn't fair; neither is monopoly tips come to those who wait
laughter is the best medicine if you don't have anything else red lights are always greener on the other side
ask not what your country can do for you because countries don't have ears all european roads lead to rome
history teachers repeat themselves shoot for the moon. if you miss, you'll die in outer space
don't be a party pooper—that's gross
if you can't beat 'em, use a stand mixer

There is one extra green word each in the left and right versions (SOTS and GUIDES). These are used in the Binoculars metapuzzle.

The first letters of the tiles with underlines spell out EMINENT/KNOWLEDGE for the left/right versions. Between these two words you can put the answer DOMAIN in the phrases EMINENT DOMAIN/DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE.
