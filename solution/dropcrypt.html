Guess Hint Puzzle

Solution: Dropcrypt
Answer: CROSS EYED

Written by Colin Lu, Jon Schneider, Anderson Wang, Robert Tunney

We are presented with two dropquotes and a blank 9×9 grid, and six blanks at the bottom. The first step is to solve the dropquotes. From the facts that 1) the cells in the dropquotes contain words instead of letters, 2) some of the entries are numbers surrounded by parentheses, suggesting word lengths, and 3) the dropquotes are labeled Across and Down, we deduce that we must put together valid clues using these words whose answers are the given lengths, and then use those answers to fill in the grid. The title suggests that these are cryptic clues, which we can confirm as we assemble some grammatically reasonable clues. Some things that help with this include:

  • In both the Across and Down dropquotes, there are 10 groups of white cells and 10 numbers in parentheses, meaning that there are 10 clues in each dropquote.
  • In particular, numbers in parentheses always go at the end of each clue (this is confirmed by noting that in each column, the number of parentheses and the number of white cells at the end of a clue are equal).
  • Some two word phrases (most prominently ULAN BATOR’S) should go together, and more generally, many sequences of words can be eliminated for being ungrammatical.

The next step is to assemble these across and down clues as a diagramless crossword. We are given that the grid is 9x9, and the across and down answers each have only 38 letters, suggesting that this is not a fully checked crossword. This is consistent with standard British-style cryptic grids, in which usually every other letter is checked.

Additionally, our clues and answers are not alphabetized, suggesting that their order matters. Indeed, the lengths of the across clues are symmetric (the first clue has the same length as the last, the 2nd has the same length as the 2nd last, etc.), suggesting that the grid has standard 180-degree rotational symmetry.
