Solution: Make Your Own Math Quiz
Answer: TRIPLE LUTZ

Written by Jakob Weisblat and Josh Alman

First, we need to write math questions that satisfy the constraints given. As the constraints reveal themselves, we may have to return to previous answers and change them. Eventually, we will come to a solution like this:

Some of the students get some of the questions wrong. We notice that all of the questions that are wrong contain letters, and we can deduce that some of the students have incorrect beliefs about the values of certain letters at certain times. In fact, each student has one letter whose value they always think is three times what it should be. These letters can be determined by cycling through different solutions to the questions using letters, such as questions 23 through 25. For students one, two, three, and four, the tripled letters are L, U, T, and Z respectively. Thus, the answer to this puzzle is TRIPLE LUTZ.

Author’s Notes

Many of the challenges in this puzzle should be familiar to teachers with experience making math quizzes: designing questions to test whether students are making common mistakes, making sure the quiz won't upset students too much, doing extra work to make sure questions have "nice" answers and constants, etc. That said, because of how weird the students' requests are, this is probably the "Make Your Own X" puzzle that least resembles the usual real-life task of making X. It's hard to imagine a student who would get upset if adjacent questions on their math quiz have large edit distance!
