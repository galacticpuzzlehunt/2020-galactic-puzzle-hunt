Solution: Watch Out!
Answer: MACBETH

Written by Yannick Yao

First, we identify the 16 images below the diagram, each corresponding to a word. (In the clear version, the enumeration of each word is given to help with identification.) These words are in alphabetical order:

  • ACROBAT
  • ARCHITECT
  • BEATLES
  • CALENDAR
  • CONSTABLE
  • DEATH
  • DHARMA (from the sitcom Dharma and Greg)
  • DYSTOPIA (the 2018 movie)
  • ESTABLISH (established with ed removed)
  • GOBSTOPPER
  • GRAMMAR
  • MENDEL (the geneticist Gregor Mendel)
  • PHARMACY
  • PROBE (a space probe)
  • PYRAMID
  • WHITE

At the top of the puzzle, there is a diagram consisting of 18 boxes and 16 sets of arrows (with ends blurred in the blurry version) in 8 colors (2 for each color). This suggests matching the arrows with the words.

At this point, we may notice that some pairs of words share long common substrings in the middle, such as DHARMA and PHARMACY, or CONSTABLE and ESTABLISH. In particular, the words can be split into 8 pairs, each pair sharing a verb that involves doing something aggressive to another party, i.e. something to “watch out” for:

  • EAT: BEATLES, DEATH
  • END: CALENDAR, MENDEL
  • HARM: DHARMA, PHARMACY
  • HIT: ARCHITECT, WHITE
  • RAM: GRAMMAR, PYRAMID
  • ROB: ACROBAT, PROBE
  • STAB: CONSTABLE, ESTABLISH
  • STOP: DYSTOPIA, GOBSTOPPER

As these verbs usually involve a subject and an object, we can parse each word such that the letter(s) before the verb are the aggressors and the letter(s) after the verb are the victims. For example, DEATH should be read as “D eat H”, and PYRAMID should be read as “P, Y ram I, D”. Therefore, we can now use the number of letters before and after the verb to determine which word corresponds to which arrow, and use the letters to fill in the boxes.

In the diagram, the arrows point from the diamond-shaped ends to the triangle-shaped ends. The different colors corresponds to different verbs:

  • Black (solid): END
  • Gray (solid): ROB
  • Blue (short dash): RAM
  • Lime (short dash): STOP
  • Pink (long dash): STAB
  • Maroon (dot): EAT
  • Red (dot + short dash): HIT
  • Cyan (dot + long dash): HARM

The letters in the middle 7 boxes spell out the answer MACBETH, a play notable for its numerous instances of stabbing, backstabbing, and revenge.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle is mostly inspired by some wordplay memes describing e.g. W hitting E, S killing S, or gobstopper protecting the society from gob. It can also be seen as a dual of the puzzle Whodunit from MITMH 2021, where the words were given as word+letter+word instead of letter(s)+word+letter(s).

In the Discord server for organizing MITMH 2021, there was a calendar bot that regularly posts schedules of upcoming meetings/deadlines. Its nickname “watch out cal” was the inspiration for the puzzle title.
