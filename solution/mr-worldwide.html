Solution: Mr. Worldwide
Answer: ONE TO ONE

Written by Max Murin, Kevin Matthew Li, Maddie Dawson

The puzzle consists of 13 song clips and 13 blurbs. The clips are presented in alphabetical order by song title, and the blurbs are presented in alphabetical order. Each song’s lyrics contain a city-to-city pair: for example, Ke$ha’s “Butterscotch” contains the words “Memphis to Bangkok.” The city-to-city pairs form a path:

Reordering the blurbs so that their clued locations appear in the order of the path, the first letters of the blurbs spell out “LISTENTOCLIPS.”

The lengths of the clips vary and are proportional to the distance of the city-to-city pair in their lyrics. We used a ratio of 66.67 miles per second and rounded clip lengths to the nearest second (approximately). For example, measuring by the location of city pins on Google Maps, Chicago and St. Louis are 262.3 miles apart, and the clip length of “Girl on the Billboard” is 4 seconds. New York and London are 3461.25 miles apart, and the clip length of “1000 Nights” is 52 seconds.

For each A-to-B city pair and the location along its line, take the ratio of the distance from A to the location to the distance from A to B. Using this ratio, find the corresponding point in the song clip, and take the word at that point. For example, the distance from New York to the House of the Seven Gables is 204.1 miles, and the distance from New York to London is 3461.25 miles, giving us a ratio of approximately 0.59. 0.59 × 52 seconds gives us 3.066; 3.066 seconds into the “1000 Nights” clip is the word “nights.”
