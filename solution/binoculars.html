Solution: Binoculars
Answer: BIRD MELODIES

Written by Jon Schneider and Anderson Wang

In this round, each puzzle has two versions, one on the left side and one on the right side. Each puzzle has a single overall solution, but two more words/phrases can be extracted from the two sides, one on the left and one on the right (see the individual puzzle solution pages for details). These are all collected in the table below:

Puzzle Answer Left word Right word
So You Think You Can Spell BUMBLEBEE REMOVAL DAY TRIP
Fridge Magnets DOMAIN SOTS GUIDES

This Puzzle Intentionally Left Blank EMPTY SET SNOWSTORMS PELTMONGERS
Intersections TRIAD GARLIC DISGUISE
Symbols MINOR INFLUENCE WOO

Much like how the left and right telescopes of binoculars show the same image from a slightly different position, each pair of left and right words are synonyms of a pair of words that are identical, except an “L” turns into an “R” and changes position. Appropriately, the left words contain the “L” and the right words contain the “R”.

We notice two things:

  • The distances between the “L” and “R” range from 1–6, with each value being used once.
  • The length of the words in the pair equals the length of the puzzle answer.

The first observation lets us sort the answers, and the second observation means we can index the positions of the L and R into the puzzle answer. To continue the strained binoculars metaphor, this is like how the images from the two telescopes are further apart when you look at closer objects, and are subsequently combined by the brain into a single 3D image.

The answer is BIRD MELODIES.

Author’s Notes

I don’t remember much about the writing of this meta anymore, but I do remember at some point in brainstorming we tried playing with L/Rs, and at another point we looked into moving letters in words (like parallax). Alan then suggested “what about both?” and this puzzle was born.
