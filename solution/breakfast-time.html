Solution: Breakfast Time!
Answer: ONLY CONNECT

Written by Jakob Weisblat; art by Jess Xu

We are presented with 5 breakfast foods we need to eat—er, solve. We’ll discuss each one individually and then the overall puzzle’s solution.

Toast

Each line on the toast has a morse code letter hidden in the middle of it (shown in green below).

Morse Letter
- - . G
. - A
- - M
. E

Translating the Morse code to letters, we get GAME.

Eggs

Each egg clues a series of progressively shorter words, with a ? at the end. Once we solve enough of the clues, we can notice that the words are contained within one another, like eggs. For example, CLASSIC contains LASSI contains ASS contains S.

The four eggs are:

  • C(L(A(S)S)I)C
  • B(O(T(H)E)R)S
  • B(A(L(O)N)E)Y
  • F(L(O(W)E)R)Y

The center letters spell out SHOW.

Pancakes

Each pair of pancakes clues a pair of words where one is missing a letter:

  • WINK INK
  • STAIR STAR
  • PINT PIN
  • BATH BAT

Taking the letters dropped from the second words, in order, we get WITH.

Waffle

The numbers on the waffle represent four games of minesweeper with slightly adjusted rules. We need to place mines such that each square’s number indicates the number of mines on adjacent (but not diagonally adjacent, or across the waffle boundary, or itself) squares.

For the top left waffle, we know that neither the zeros nor the ones that are adjacent to them can have any syrup (mines) placed in them. Thus, the ones in A1, A2, D4 and C4 have only one adjacent cell each that can have a syrup (mine) in them. So cells A1, B1, D3 and D4 have syrup. Now C3 has its one adjacent syrup, so C2 can’t have any syrup in it, and we need to put syrup in C1, D1 and D2 to finish the waffle.

For the bottom left waffle, the corners each have exactly 2 adjacent cells, so they must both be filled with syrup. Now the four cells in the middle have all met their constraints, so none of their additional neighbors (each other) can have syrup. That means we need to put syrup on the corners to finish the constraints from the edge cells.

For the top right waffle, we start with the threes in A6, A7, D6 and D7. They each have exactly three neighbors, and so all three of them must have syrup. Once we fill in those 12 cells, all the cells have their constraints met, so we cannot add any more syrup and the waffle is complete.

Finally, for the bottom right waffle, we start with the threes in G8 and F5. We fill in their three neighbors with syrup. Now, consider the 2 in F8 and the 1 in E7. They share two neighbors, at most one of which can be filled with syrup. Thus, the other neighbor of the 2, G8, must be filled with syrup. Following the same logic with the mirror pair of 1 and two in G5 and H6, we can fill F5 with syrup. Now, G6 and H7 cannot be filled with syrup because G7 has its constraint satisfied, so the remaining syrup to satisfy H6 must go in H5. Using similar logic, we can deduce the last syrup goes in E8.

Looking at the completed grid, we see that the syrup spells out the word LION.

Fries

We start by solving the clues on the two fries provided:

Clue Answer
“That was close!” WHEW
Game franchise since 2001 HALO
He sang about a pickle ARLO
Breakfast, e.g. MEAL
Punch sound WHAM
Loser of a famous race HARE
“The first lady of song” ELLA
Shepherd’s product WOOL

At some point while solving the clues, we may notice that they are four letters long and can be written in the holes of the remaining fry. We only have enough room to write 16 letters, which means the letters must be shared between the answers. In fact, the same set of letters are used in both sets of answers, one down and one across. This is a crossword puzzle.

Reading the diagonal of the crossword grid (represented above in two ways), we get WALL.

Breakfast Time!

Once we’ve eaten all the elements of the breakfast, we can put it all together. The clue phrase is GAME SHOW WITH LION WALL. If we need an extra nudge, the top of the unblurred menu mentions a full English breakfast. The game show with a lion wall, which happens to be English, is ONLY CONNECT, the answer to this puzzle.

Author’s Notes

At some point during brainstorming for the hunt, Abby suggested that we have a round of even easier puzzles before our first ‘intro’ round of easier puzzles. That round didn’t make it into the final plans, so I wrote this puzzle in the hopes of having some accessible minipuzzles early on in the hunt.
