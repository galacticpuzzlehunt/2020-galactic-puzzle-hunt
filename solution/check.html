Solution: ✅
Answer: CALM

Written by Anderson Wang, Ben Yang, Brian Chen, Lennart Jansson

We can solve the crossword clues. It’s a diagramless barred crossword without any symmetry. The four unclued objects to place on the grid are all chess pieces, which helps disambiguate them and explains why the crossword is 8×8, like a chessboard.

Across

  1. Frame used in papermaking : DECKLE
  2. Borden mascot : ELSIE
  3. “Mad ___: Fury Road” : MAX
  4. First object to place on the grid : KNIGHT
  5. “At ___ rate” : ANY
  6. Implied : GOT AT (as a transitive verb)
  7. 15-Down, for example : FISH
  8. Second object to place on the grid : PAWN
  9. Modern suffix with spam or chat : BOT
  10. Chocolate source : CACAO
  11. Fourth object to place on the grid : BISHOP
  12. Like some Halloween decorations : EERIE
  13. Milk source : SOY

Down

  1. It won’t give you a buzz : DECAF
  2. Antlered animal : ELK
  3. Third object to place on the grid : KING
  4. Floral garland : LEI
  5. Diagnostic for muscles and neurons (abbr.): EMG
  6. “Funny!” : HAHA
  7. Added numbers, briefly? : EXT (numbers that are added to phone numbers)
  8. What’s up? : SKY
  9. Boastful mother in Greek myth : NIOBE
  10. Lampridae member : OPAH
  11. Hot dogs and slices of pie, per the cube rule : TACOS (per The Cube Rule of Food Identification)
  12. Hubbub : STIR
  13. Publication owned by Jeff Bezos, informally : WAPO (Washington Post)
  14. Prying : NOSY
  15. Insect that should not be able to fly, per a 2007 movie : BEE (referencing the opening lines of the Bee Movie)
  16. Show with a “Cyber” spinoff : CSI

The complete 8×8 grid looks as follows:

DECKLEHE
ELSIEMAX
CKKNIGHT
ANYGOTAT
FISHPAWN
BOTCACAO
EBISHOPS
EERIESOY

In crossword terminology, a square is “checked” if it is part of both an across and a down answer, and “unchecked” if it is only part of one (either across or down). The unchecked letters read CHECK THESE. We must place the four chess pieces onto the board so that all of the squares not checked in the crossword are “checked” (i.e., attacked) by the chess pieces. There is only one way to do this.

DECKLEHE
E3LSIE4M2AX
CKKNIGHT
ANYGOTAT
FISHPAWN
BOTCA1CAO
EBISHOPS
EERIESOY

(The easiest way to see this is probably to observe that the king, knight, and bishop can all attack at most 3 of the squares, and the pawn can attack at most 1, even if they are placed completely independently. So all pieces must attack the maximum number of squares they can, which already uniquely places the king and knight. After that, the bishop is immediately uniquely placed as well, and the pawn must be placed to attack the remaining corner square.)

The clues for the chess pieces tell us their ordering. Reading the letters on the squares corresponding to each piece’s position gives:

  1. First: Knight → C
  2. Second: Pawn → A
  3. Third: King → L
  4. Fourth: Bishop → M

The answer is CALM.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle was made when the four of us were group brainstorming and Brian mentioned a puzzle title idea of "Oops! All Unchecked Squares" (without any idea of the actual mechanics). Ben suggested the chess pun, and the rest of the idea formed pretty quickly from there. We originally planned on providing the chess pieces separately, but surprisingly had enough flexibility to fit them in the grid itself.
