Solution: Seal of Quality
Answer: ONE-DOLLAR BILL

Written by Alan Huang

The body of this puzzle consists of thirteen (rather bastardized) heraldic descriptions. If we start drawing some of them out, though, we may notice elements like a space habitat or robot factory that don’t belong on actual period shields or coats of arms.

In fact, each image is actually the box art for a video game — specifically, one prominently featuring a main character in the center. That character’s name is replaced with its enumeration in parentheses, and one additional word in each description is omitted.

As hinted at by the flavortext, the descriptions do not include any of the text in the art (like the game titles). In alphabetical order, the games are:

Per bend … head The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess Link

At the bottom, we can replace each character’s initials with their corresponding word (there are two Ms and two Zs, but they can be disambiguated using grammar):

Vert; on an orle sable, three cartouches and an escutcheon at the corners, separated by leaves; within, a large cartouche sable, bearing (6 10)’s head, flanked by two roundels indented.

With the character this time being George Washington, this roughly describes the United States ONE-DOLLAR BILL.
