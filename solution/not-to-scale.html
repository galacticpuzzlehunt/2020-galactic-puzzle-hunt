Guess Hint Puzzle

Solution: Not to Scale
Answer: SAINT NICK

Written by Chris Jones and Colin Lu

In this puzzle we have to cut and fold the given PDF so that the dark regions are hidden inside the finished origami model and the non-dark regions are on the outside. When finished, each model looks like a famous piece of architecture.

The clue on a model clues something about the piece of architecture. Index the number into the clue answer to get a letter, and order these letters by the height of the piece of architecture (since they are ''not to scale'').

Finished model Landmark Height Clue Clue answer Letter

Kaaba		 13m Number of laps seven S

Christ the Redeemer		 38m Mountain Corcovado A

Sydney Opera House1		 65m One million of them cover the shell tiles I

Great Pyramid		 147m Middle Ages misnomer Joseph's granary N

Space Needle2		 184m City Seattle T

Golden Gate Bridge3		 227m Official color international orange N

Eiffel Tower		 300m Material (one word) iron I

Petronas Towers		 452m Park (abbr.) KLCC Park C

Taipei 1014		 509m Surpasser Burj Khalifa K

The answer is SAINT NICK (who also matches the flavor text description of a “builder of tiny things”, toys).

  1. The incredible one-fold Sydney Opera House is inspired by an origami meme, the one-fold stegosaurus. ↩

  2. The top of the Space Needle is a type of double sink fold where the top pokes out (though most solvers probably squashed paper inside until it looked good enough). To fold it, crease the top square into an 8x8 grid of small squares, and then do a double sink. ↩

  3. The bridge towers can be folded before or after folding the left and right triangles in; after looks better. ↩

  4. At the base of each trapezoid are tiny squash folds. To fold the hexagon at the top, start by folding along the upper left and upper right edges of the hexagon (these two lines should meet at the top). ↩

Author’s Notes

Chris: This was my first experience folding origami, and it was really fun! The idea to “fold dark regions inside” seems to work really well for simple origami models like these. For more complex models, it becomes very hard (but solutions still seem fairly unique).

The PDFs were designed in Figma. In the first test of this puzzle, some of the models left a lot to be desired. Here are the first Space Needle, Eiffel Tower, and Taipei 101.
