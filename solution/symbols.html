Solution: Symbols
Answer: MINOR

Written by Brian Chen, Chris Jones, Patrick Xia

First we solve the clues. The answers have many ON and IN substrings, which helps resolve the (often highly ambiguous) clues.

Each answer corresponds to a textual description of one of the symbols as follows:

  • The prepositions represent themselves, where “X IN Y” means “X enclosed by Y” and “X ON Y” means “X stacked vertically above Y”. Prepositions are left-associative: for example, L ON G IN G is (L ON G) IN G instead of L ON (G IN G).
  • Each consonant, including Y, represents a different “noun”, some kind of two-dimensional outlined shape.
  • Each vowel represents a different “adjective”, which modifies the “noun” immediately after it, either changing its appearance (crossed-out or filled-in) or multiplicity (two or three copies).

The answer-symbol correspondence also helps resolve ambiguities, especially for the longer answers.

Translating the symbol back into the language of the puzzle produces M IN OR. The answer is MINOR.

Left/right answer extraction

The left and right versions of the puzzles differ only in that some numbers are red. If we match the clues to the resulting answer order (as we did in the second table above), reading the first letters of the clues corresponding to the red numbers extracts the left and right answers INFLUENCE and WOO.
